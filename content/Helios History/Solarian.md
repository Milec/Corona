## Overview

A Solarian is a being who has awakened the ability to absorb, channel, and weaponize stellar radiation — drawing power from the light of stars and expressing it through physical combat, gravitational manipulation, and the direct emission of solar energy. The class originates in the *Starfinder* system and exists across many races, though it is exceedingly rare in most species. Among the [[Helian Race]], Solarians are far more prevalent than in any other known population, a consequence of the [[Helion Nodes]] that define Helian biology.

Solarian ability is not a separate power grafted onto the body. It is an expression of the self taken past a threshold most people never approach — a fundamental alignment between a living being and the processes that govern stars.

---

## Prevalence by Race

Solarian manifestation has been documented across multiple species, but the rates vary enormously. Among most races, a Solarian is a generational anomaly — something that occurs perhaps once in a civilization's history without explanation. The ability is not understood to be hereditary in non-Helian species, and no pattern of manifestation has been established.

Among the [[Helian Race]], manifestation rates are dramatically higher. This is attributed to the [[Helion Nodes]] — biological structures that already process stellar radiation at the cellular level, creating an existing biological framework that Solarian awakening can build on rather than construct from nothing. Helians do not automatically become Solarians, but they are far more likely to than any other known species.

> *Historically, a meaningful portion of the Helian population carried latent Solarian aptitude. This changed after the [[Helios History/Imperial Conquest|Algalterian conquest]].*

## Solar Color

A Solarian's color is fixed at birth. It is visible in the hue of their corona when drawing significantly on their abilities, and in subtle undertones of their light emission at all times. Color reflects the nature, character, and potential ceiling of their power — and follows the spectral lifecycle of stars, from formation through death.

The spectrum runs from coolest to hottest, youngest to most extreme. A Solarian's color determines how powerful they can become through practice, it shapes the trajectory of their abilities and, among Helians, influences the lifespan curve they will follow.

**🔴 Red Sun**

The lowest expression of Solarian potential. Red Suns possess comparatively limited output, with abilities that manifest slowly and favor density, endurance, and gravitational influence over destructive force. Their power is stable and difficult to extinguish, but lacks the overwhelming intensity of the higher spectra.

**🟠 Orange Sun**

A moderate manifestation of Solarian capability. Orange Suns produce greater thermal and kinetic output than Red Suns while maintaining strong stability and control. They are regarded as reliable and resilient, capable of sustaining prolonged combat or exertion without the volatility seen in more powerful colors.

**🟡 Yellow Sun**

The historical standard of Helian Solarian power and the most common manifestation among the noble bloodlines. Yellow Suns represent the midpoint of the spectrum: powerful enough to vastly surpass ordinary Helians, yet stable enough to refine through discipline and training. Most of the legendary Solarians of Helian history were Yellow Suns.

**🟢 Green Sun**

A high-order manifestation associated with advanced control and heightened solar perception. Green Suns possess greater output than Yellow Suns, though their strength is often expressed through precision rather than scale. Their command over energy manipulation, sensory range, and refined application of force makes them exceptionally dangerous despite lacking the raw intensity of the upper spectrum.

**🔵 Blue Sun**

An extremely rare manifestation representing the upper limits of confirmed Solarian evolution. Blue Suns generate vastly greater energy output than all lower spectra and typically awaken earlier and more violently. Their presence alone is often enough to destabilize surrounding environments through heat, pressure, and radiant force. The bloodline programs of the [[Helios Hegemony]] were primarily dedicated to producing Blue Suns, with only limited success across millennia.

## Abilities in Play

Solarian abilities in play are governed by the interplay between two opposing states — **Photon** and **Graviton** — which the Solarian shifts between through their actions in and out of combat.

**Photon Mode** — The Solarian attunes to the emissive, expansive nature of stars. In Photon attunement, abilities emphasize light, heat, radiation, and explosive force. Fully attuned Photon Solarians burn — their strikes carry solar energy, their presence is visibly radiant, and their offensive output is at its peak.

**Graviton Mode** — The Solarian attunes to the contracting, pulling nature of stellar gravity. In Graviton attunement, abilities emphasize mass, control, spatial distortion, and defense. Fully attuned Graviton Solarians bend — they pull enemies, redirect force, and operate as the center of mass around which a fight is organized.

**Zenith Revelations** — When fully attuned to either mode, the Solarian gains access to their most powerful abilities. These represent the star at its most extreme expression in either direction — maximum emission or maximum collapse. Each Zenith ability is tied to the Solarian's current stellar stage and becomes more devastating as they advance.

**The Solar Weapon / Solar Armor** — Solarians manifest either a weapon or armor of solid stellar energy. The weapon shifts form with attunement — burning and aggressive in Photon, dense and controlled in Graviton. This manifestation is fundamental to the class and cannot be replicated by technology. It is one of the primary ways a trained Solarian can identify an artificial emulation.

**Stellar Revelations** — Abilities unlocked at each level that reflect the Solarian's current stellar stage. As the stage advances, earlier revelations are superseded by more powerful expressions of the same underlying phenomena.

## Stellar Stages & Helian Lifespan

Among non-Helian Solarians, advancement through stellar stages has no documented effect on natural lifespan. Among the [[Helian Race]], it does — dramatically.

Helian Solarian lifespan follows a bell curve tied to stellar stage. Advancement through the early and middle stages extends lifespan far beyond even the Helian baseline. Advancement into the late stages begins to consume it. The peak is **Main Sequence Prime** — a Solarian at this stage may live between 1,000 and 2,000 years. Past that peak, each further stage shortens the remaining span as the Solarian's Helion Nodes begin to burn through their biological substrate in the process of sustaining greater and greater output.

| Level | Stellar Stage | Helian Lifespan |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | Protostar | 80–120 yrs |
| 3–4 | T Tauri | 150–250 yrs |
| 5–7 | Main Sequence — Young | 400–700 yrs |
| 8–10 | Main Sequence — Prime *(Peak)* | 1,000–2,000 yrs |
| 11–13 | Subgiant | 600–900 yrs |
| 14–16 | Red Giant | 300–500 yrs |
| 17–18 | Helium Flash | 100–150 yrs |
| 19 | White Dwarf / Supernova | 40–60 yrs |
| 20 | Stellar Remnant | 20–30 yrs |

> *A level 20 Solarian may be 1,400 years old with only 20–30 years remaining. They have spent most of their life reaching this point.*

The lifespan shown reflects the Solarian's total remaining expected lifespan at that stage, not years added. A Solarian who advances past Prime does not lose the years they have already lived — they lose the years they had ahead of them.

## Stalling — The Cost of Refusing to Advance

A Solarian can delay advancing to the next stellar stage. This is sometimes chosen deliberately — a Solarian who has reached Main Sequence Prime and wishes to remain there for as long as possible, or one who fears what the late stages will cost them. Other Solarians view this choice with cultural contempt, considering it equivalent to a warrior refusing to fight.

Several rulings govern this:

**The Star Doesn't Lie** — The universe pushes Solarians to grow. Stalling creates instability — the stellar process is ongoing whether the Solarian participates in it or not. Refusing advancement may carry debuffs or psychological costs as the internal pressure builds. A star fighting its own gravity is not stable.

**Coasting Is Noticed** — Other Solarians can tell when someone is stalling. The corona reads differently. The attunement is off. This carries cultural stigma among orders like [[The Eclipse Knights]], where the refusal to advance is treated as a kind of cowardice.

**Hard Cap** — A Solarian can delay a stage transition, but only for a set period — approximately 50 years per level — before the fusion process forces the change regardless. The body does not grant indefinite extension.

## Death — The Supernova

When a Solarian dies, they do not simply stop. The stellar energy that has been building within them throughout their life — channeled, accumulated, and held — releases all at once.

The release takes the form of a supernova-scale explosion of solar energy, the magnitude of which scales directly with the Solarian's level and stellar stage at the time of death. A Protostar Solarian's death is dangerous to those immediately nearby. A Stellar Remnant Solarian's death is an event with a radius measured in geography.

This is not a chosen ability. It is a consequence of what a Solarian is — the accumulated stellar pressure of a lifetime released in a single uncontrolled moment. It cannot be suppressed, redirected, or withheld. A Solarian who knows they are dying has, at most, enough time to tell the people near them to run.

The Eclipse Knights account for this in their doctrine. No Solarian is permitted to die in a populated area if it can be prevented. This is not sentiment — it is physics.

> *A sufficiently advanced Solarian's death is indistinguishable from a stellar event. The distinction matters only to those outside the blast radius.*

## Manifestation

### Historical (Pre-Conquest)
Before the Algalterian conquest, Solarian manifestation among Helians was not exclusive to the nobility. A significant portion of the general population carried latent aptitude. The conditions that triggered awakening — environment, circumstance, and what fragmentary accounts describe as a threshold state of *readiness* — are no longer known within the Hegemony. The knowledge was deliberately destroyed in the purge; what survived of it, survived only with the exiles who carried it beyond Imperial reach and became [[The Eclipse Knights]].

Noble bloodlines manifested more frequently and more powerfully on average, which is why selective breeding produced results. But average was not exclusive.

### Current Era (Post-Conquest)
Helians born in the modern era are unaware of their natural potential. Even among the noble class, attempting to awaken Solarian power is forbidden — it violates [[The Eclipse Treaty]] and would invite consequences no house is willing to court.

No natural-born true Solarian has been allowed to exist since the first emperor. On the rare occasion a child shows genuine aptitude, they are culled before they can mature into a usurper of the noble status quo — which is precisely why the survival of one, hidden and smuggled off-world, is the quiet catastrophe the entire post-conquest arrangement exists to prevent.

### Artificial Emulation
The technology distributed to modern [[Heir Candidacy System|heir candidates]] replicates the visual signature of Yellow Sun Photon attunement convincingly. A corona appears. Thermal output is produced. Physical augmentation is partial.

What it cannot replicate: Graviton attunement. The solar weapon's weight and behavior under stress. The sensation other Solarians perceive when near genuine stellar energy. And the death supernova — artificial emulation does not accumulate. It does not release.

A trained Solarian can identify emulation immediately. An ordinary observer cannot.

## Historical Suppression

The Algalterian Empire's categorical hostility to Solarian ability was practical as much as ideological. Solarian warriors were the primary reason the [[Helios History/Imperial Conquest|wars of conquest]] were as difficult as they were. The Empire did not misunderstand what it was dealing with — it understood exactly, and moved to ensure it would never face it again.

The destruction of manifestation knowledge, the purge of known Solarians, and the installation of artificial emulation as a replacement were not cultural vandalism. They were strategic decisions. The Hegemony was permitted to keep the appearance of its solar tradition because the appearance posed no threat. The reality was eliminated. 

> *The exact conditions under which Solarians historically manifested are no longer known, even among the modern nobility who inherited the system that replaced them.*

## Notable Solarians

- **[[Marcus]]** (A-7527, *Corona*) — Blue Sun, anomalous manifestation from birth. The only confirmed genuine Solarian of the current era. Born into [[House Bellator]]'s cycle, exiled as a child, trained by [[The Eclipse Knights]] to Squire rank, currently imprisoned by the Algalterian Empire.
- **[[The First Emperor]]** — the first and only *other* Blue Sun in recorded Helian history; founder of the [[Caerulius Bloodline]] and the [[Helios Hegemony]], millennia before Marcus.
- **[[Phoenix]]** — a Green Sun Blade of the Eclipse Knights, and the knight who trained Marcus.
