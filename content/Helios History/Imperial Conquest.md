## Overview

The Imperial Conquest refers to the series of wars fought between the [[Helios Hegemony]] and the [[Algalterian Galactic Empire]] several hundred years ago — a conflict that ended in the total subjugation of the Helian people and the permanent transformation of their civilization.

The wars killed millions. The betrayal that ended them cost more.

---

## Background

At the time of first contact, the Hegemony was a formidable star system power — controlling colonies and installations across every major body in the [[Helios System]]. Its military was among the finest in its region of space, its noble bloodlines had been refined for millennia toward martial and intellectual excellence, and its [[Solarian]] warriors represented a class of combatant that conventional Imperial forces found genuinely difficult to counter.

The Algalterian Galactic Empire had never encountered Solarian ability before. Its institutional culture despised anything it categorized as mystical — treating such abilities as dangerous, and incompatible with Imperial order. This contempt did not prevent the Empire from recognizing the threat. It simply shaped how they chose to address it.

---

## The Wars

The conflict unfolded across multiple campaigns spanning decades. The Empire's advantages were scale, industrial output, and strategic patience. The Hegemony's advantages were Solarian warriors, intimate knowledge of their own system, and a nobility that — at first — was unified in resistance.

Early campaigns favored neither side cleanly. Helian Solarian units were devastating in direct engagement but could not be everywhere at once. The Empire absorbed losses that would have broken smaller powers and kept advancing, grinding through the outer system colonies one by one, cutting supply lines, and isolating Helios Prime from its territories.

As the wars dragged on and the outer colonies fell, fractures began forming within the [[Patrilium]]. Several noble houses, calculating that the Empire could not ultimately be stopped, began making quiet assessments of what survival might cost.

---

## The Betrayal

Near the end of the conflict, a faction of Helian nobility entered into secret negotiations with the Empire. The terms were straightforward: in exchange for Imperial recognition of their titles, continued governance of their continental domains, and protection from the post-war purge, they would provide the Empire with the intelligence and access needed to end Solarian resistance.

They delivered.

The last Hegemon who refused to capitulate was deposed. The last loyal generals were isolated and destroyed. The Solarian warriors who had held the Empire's advance for decades were systematically identified, targeted, and eliminated — hunted down with the assistance of the people they had been fighting to protect.

A small number escaped. The last loyal king — aware of what was coming and unable to stop it — arranged for some of his most capable Solarians to be sent beyond Imperial reach before the final collapse. These exiles would eventually become the foundation of [[The Eclipse Knights]].

---

## The Purge

Following the formal end of hostilities, the Empire moved to permanently eliminate Solarian ability from Helian civilization. The purge was methodical: known Solarians were executed, records of manifestation conditions were destroyed or seized, and the knowledge of how true solar power arose was deliberately eradicated from Helian culture.

The Empire had no interest in understanding Solarian ability. It wanted it gone.

What the Empire could not fully account for was the [[Helion Nodes]] — the biological structures underlying all Helian potential. These could not simply be removed. But with the knowledge of awakening destroyed, the Empire calculated that dormant potential was no threat.

They were, for several hundred years, correct.

---

## The Rewriting

With the purge complete and the collaborating nobility installed as the new ruling class, the Empire assisted in the construction of a revised history. The population was taught that:

- Solar powers had always been exclusive to the noble caste
- Those powers are rare even amongst the nobles
- The current noble families were the rightful and ancient stewards of Helian civilization

Technology capable of emulating Solarian abilities was distributed to the nobility to maintain the appearance of divine inheritance. The [[Heir Candidacy System]] was restructured around this technology, and the [[Imperial Offering]] cycle continued as before — with the crucial difference that the powers displayed within it were now manufactured.

The collaborating houses prospered. The population accepted the history they were given. And the Hegemony continued — smaller, quieter, and thoroughly controlled.

---

## Legacy

The wars ended with [[The Eclipse Treaty]].

The conquest's most durable wound is not territorial. The outer system colonies are gone, but they are remembered. What is not remembered — cannot be remembered, by design — is what the Helian people once were before the Empire decided what they should be.

Should a Solarian ever manifest within the race again, it is understood — and agreed — that they are to be executed.
