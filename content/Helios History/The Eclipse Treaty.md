## Overview

The Eclipse Treaty is the formal agreement that ended the wars between the [[Helios Hegemony]] and the [[Algalterian Galactic Empire]] and defined the terms under which the Hegemony continues to exist. It was signed by the collaborating nobility on behalf of a people who were not consulted, and its terms have governed Helian civilization ever since.

The treaty takes its name from the eclipse — the moment when something bright is obscured by something larger. Whether this was an Imperial joke, a Helian mourning, or simply a coincidence of timing has been debated and lost to history.

---

## Terms

**Territorial Concession**
The Hegemony formally surrendered all colonies, installations, and territorial claims beyond [[Helios Prime]] itself. The outer system — every station, colony, and outpost the Hegemony had built across its star system at the height of its civilization — passed into Imperial administration. Helios Prime and its immediate orbital space were permitted to remain under Hegemony governance.

**Nominal Sovereignty**
The Hegemony was permitted to retain its internal governmental structure — the [[Caerulius Bloodline]] throne, the [[Patrilium]], the [[Matrilium]], the [[Heir Candidacy System]], and [[The Seven Noble Dynasties]]. The Empire does not govern the Hegemony directly. It does not need to.

**Imperial Oversight**
An [[The Imperial Observers|Imperial observer presence]] was established on Helios Prime. Officially advisory. In practice, any action taken by the Hegemony that conflicts with Imperial interests is subject to Imperial review and reversal. The distinction between nominal sovereignty and genuine governance has narrowed steadily in the centuries since the treaty was signed.

**The Solarian Clause**
The treaty's most consequential and least publicly acknowledged provision. Any Helian found to possess genuine Solarian abilities — as opposed to the technologically emulated abilities sanctioned for use by heir candidates — is to be reported to Imperial authorities. The handling of such individuals is the Empire's prerogative, not the Hegemony's.

This clause exists because the Empire does not trust the collaborating nobility to eliminate true Solarians reliably. The nobility agreed to it because refusing would have ended the negotiation.

**The Eclipse Knights Provision**
The order of exiled Solarians that formed beyond Imperial space — [[The Eclipse Knights]] — presented a specific problem during negotiations. They were beyond Imperial reach, they were organized, and moving against them directly would be costly and disruptive.

The treaty addresses this obliquely: the Hegemony formally disavows the Eclipse Knights and any responsibility for their actions. The Empire formally agrees not to treat Eclipse Knight activity as grounds for punitive action against the Hegemony, provided the Hegemony cooperates with Imperial intelligence efforts regarding the order.

In practice, this provision created a permanent cold arrangement: the Empire monitors the Eclipse Knights, the Hegemony pretends they do not exist, and neither side moves openly against the other. The Eclipse Knights, for their part, operate in the knowledge that direct confrontation with the Empire would end them — and limit themselves accordingly.

---

## Current Status

The Eclipse Treaty remains in effect. Its terms are not publicly circulated within the Hegemony — the population is aware that a treaty exists and that it ended the wars, but its specific provisions, particularly the Solarian Clause and the Eclipse Knights Provision, are not common knowledge.

The treaty is reviewed by Imperial administration on a generational basis. No significant renegotiation has occurred. The Hegemony has not been in a position to request one.

---

## The Arrest of Marcus

The imprisonment of [[Marcus]] — an Eclipse Knight squire and genuine Solarian — falls precisely at the intersection of the Solarian Clause and the Eclipse Knights Provision. The Empire chose imprisonment over execution in part because execution would constitute a provocation under the spirit of the Eclipse Knights Provision. His continued imprisonment is legally ambiguous and politically calculated.
