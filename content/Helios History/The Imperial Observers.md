## Overview

The [[Algalterian Galactic Empire]] does not garrison [[Helios Prime]]. It watches it. The Imperial observer presence — established under the [[The Eclipse Treaty]] and officially advisory — is the Empire's eye inside the Hegemony, and the quiet proof of who truly holds it.

Most Helians will never knowingly see one. They know only that the observers exist, that they are not to be questioned, and that behind the old thrones and rituals sits something colder that does not need to raise its voice.

---

## Advisory in Name

The treaty grants the Empire the right of oversight. In principle, the observers advise; in practice, any action the Hegemony takes that conflicts with Imperial interest is subject to their review and reversal. Across the centuries since the treaty was signed, the distinction between advising the throne and commanding it has narrowed to almost nothing.

The [[Caerulius Bloodline]] still reigns. The [[Patrilium]] and [[Matrilium]] still convene. The observers simply make certain none of it matters in the ways the Empire cares about.

---

## What They Watch For

Above all else, the observers exist to enforce the [[The Eclipse Treaty|Solarian Clause]]. The Empire does not trust the collaborating nobility to eliminate true Solarians reliably, and so keeps its own eyes on the problem: any genuine [[Solarian]] is to be reported to Imperial authority, and their handling is the Empire's prerogative, not the Hegemony's.

They are, in the end, the mechanism by which a true Solarian like [[Marcus]] passes out of Helian hands and into Imperial ones. What they already know about the current cycle — whether the nobility feeds them everything, or hides what it dares — and what they would do if they learned a Blue Sun had been born and lost on their watch, are questions the Hegemony would very much prefer never to test.
