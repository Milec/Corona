## Overview

The Algalterian Galactic Empire is the vast interstellar power that conquered the [[Helios Hegemony]] several hundred years ago and has held it as a vassal ever since. Where the Hegemony was a single-system civilization — formidable, refined, and ancient — the Empire is something larger and colder: an industrial state spanning many worlds, governed by patience, scale, and an institutional certainty that it is the natural order of things.

To the people of [[Helios Prime]], the Empire is less a nation than a fact of the sky. Most Helians have never seen an Algalterian. They know only that a treaty exists, that it ended the wars, and that the observers who occasionally pass through the high courts are not to be questioned.

---

## Character of the Empire

The Empire's defining trait is contempt for the mystical. Its institutional culture treats anything it cannot measure, manufacture, or command as a threat to order — and it categorized [[Solarian]] ability, from first contact, as precisely that kind of threat. This was not superstition. The Empire understood exactly what it was facing. Its response was simply to decide that such a thing should not be permitted to exist.

Against the Hegemony's solar warriors and refined bloodlines, the Empire brought advantages that did not tire: overwhelming industrial output, effectively limitless manpower, and a strategic patience that could absorb losses which would have broken a smaller power. It did not need to win quickly. It only needed to keep advancing.

---

## Rule of the Hegemony

The Empire does not govern [[Helios Prime]] directly. It does not need to. Through the [[The Eclipse Treaty|Eclipse Treaty]] it installed a collaborating nobility as the visible ruling class, permitted the old institutions — the [[Caerulius Bloodline]] throne, the [[Patrilium]], the [[Matrilium]], the [[Heir Candidacy System]] — to continue in form, and hollowed them of any power that could threaten Imperial interests.

An Imperial observer presence remains on Helios Prime, officially advisory. In practice, any action of the Hegemony that conflicts with Imperial interest is subject to review and reversal. The distinction between nominal sovereignty and genuine control has narrowed steadily across the centuries since the treaty was signed.

---

## The Solarian Problem

The Empire's single unbending demand is the elimination of true Solarian ability. It does not trust the collaborating nobility to enforce this reliably, and so wrote the obligation directly into the [[The Eclipse Treaty|treaty]]: any genuine Solarian is to be reported to Imperial authority, and their handling is the Empire's prerogative — not the Hegemony's.

It is this arrangement that governs the fate of [[Marcus]]. The Empire chose to hold him as Inmate A-7527 rather than execute him — not out of mercy, but because the political cost of killing a known [[The Eclipse Knights|Eclipse Knight]] with authentic Solarian power was, for now, judged higher than the cost of keeping him in a containment field.

---

## The Eclipse Knights

Beyond Imperial reach, the exiled order of [[The Eclipse Knights]] presents a problem the Empire has chosen to manage rather than solve. The order is not strong enough to threaten the Empire directly, and rooting it out of the outer fringe would be costly and disruptive. So the two sides hold a permanent cold arrangement: the Empire monitors the order, the Hegemony pretends it does not exist, and neither moves openly against the other so long as the balance holds.
