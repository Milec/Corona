## Overview

Every succession cycle, the [[Imperial Offering]] produces forty-nine children — seven from each of [[The Seven Noble Dynasties|the seven houses]] — and the [[Heir Candidacy System]] sets them against one another for the Solar Throne. The forty-nine of the current cycle are the world [[Marcus]] was born into, and the one he vanished from.

They are, at once, a family and a field of rivals: bound to a single throne by blood, and raised to understand that only one of them will keep it.

---

## Raised Together, Set Apart

From the age of three, the candidates enter the academy system as a single cohort — drilled in strategy, history, mathematics, diplomacy, combat, etiquette, and military doctrine, and measured against one another before they are old enough to understand the stakes. Competition is expected. Mercy is not. Noble courts are polished things, but they were never kind, and the children learn to smile before they strike.

Some of the forty-nine grow close in spite of it. Marcus endured much of his childhood through the companionship of a handful of children from [[House Aegis]] and [[House Vox]] — most of whose names have long since faded from his memory.

One never did.

---

## The One Who Left the Count

Two names of the current cycle carry weight in this record. [[Juno Caerulius Aurum Prima|Juno]] of [[House Aurum]] — a leading candidate, and the girl Marcus loved. And Marcus himself: the seventh child of the seventh house, who by every projection should never have stood above the others, and who by his twelfth year was becoming the likely heir — until the night the Bellator estate burned and the count quietly became forty-eight.

The sanctioned history records a candidate lost to a tragic fire. It does not record what he was. Most of the surviving forty-nine believe the boy is simply dead; whether any of them glimpsed the blue light that night, and kept it to themselves, is not written anywhere.
