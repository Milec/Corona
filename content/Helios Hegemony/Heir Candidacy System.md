## Overview

The Heir Candidacy System is the formal process by which a successor to the [[Caerulius Bloodline]] throne is identified and appointed. Though its form has changed dramatically since the Algalterian conquest, the core principle has always been the same: candidates compete, and only the strongest ascends.

---

## Historical Process (Pre-Conquest)

Before the conquest, heir candidacy was inseparable from genuine [[Solarian]] ability. All 49 children born of the [[Imperial Offering]] were potential candidates — and Solarian manifestation was a power that any of them could, in principle, develop.

Candidates competed through formal contests of skill, governance, combat, and displayed aptitude across every domain a future Hegemon would need to master. These competitions were public, prestigious, and brutal in their demands. A candidate who excelled in warfare but showed no capacity for statecraft was no more viable than one who could govern but not fight.

Solarian power was the most visible and celebrated marker of candidacy. When a child manifested abilities, it was treated as confirmation of divine fitness to rule — a signal to the court, the dynasties, and the population alike. Stronger manifestations carried more weight. A Blue Sun, had one ever been confirmed, would have ended the competition entirely.

Rankings shifted constantly. A candidate who led the field at fifteen could be displaced by a rival's late manifestation at seventeen. The system was designed to keep every candidate striving until the moment of appointment.

---

## The Imperial Conquest and Its Effect

The Algalterian conquest permanently altered the candidacy system in ways the modern nobility does not openly acknowledge.

Before the conquest, Solarian manifestation was a natural phenomenon — something that arose from within, through conditions that have since been lost or deliberately obscured. It was not guaranteed, but it was real, and it was available to any child with sufficient aptitude regardless of which house they came from.

After the conquest, the collaborating nobility — with Imperial assistance — suppressed and eventually eliminated the knowledge of how true manifestation occurred. In its place, they introduced technology capable of **emulating** Solarian powers. This technology was distributed selectively among heir candidates who demonstrated sufficient competence, maintaining the appearance of divine inheritance while ensuring the Empire retained ultimate leverage over who could and could not display power.

The consequence is structural: Solarian ability is no longer something a candidate develops. It is something they are **granted** — and can be revoked.

---

## Modern Process (Post-Conquest)

Under the current regime, heir candidates are still selected from among the noble families and still compete through contests of skill, governance, combat, and displayed aptitude. The competitions themselves remain largely unchanged in form.

What has changed is the nature of the power they display.

Candidates who demonstrate sufficient competence upon reaching their **18th year** are granted emulation technology and formally elevated to candidacy. Rankings remain unfixed — the standing of any candidate can change at any time at the monarch's discretion.

The population is told that solar aptitude manifests naturally in roughly 1 in 7 of the monarch's children — a ratio that conveniently matches the number of candidates typically elevated. In truth, the selection is based on political and competence assessments made well before the 18th year, and the technology is applied accordingly.

---

## Imperial Narrative (Official Fiction)

> *Solar aptitude is rare, manifesting in roughly 1 in 7 of the monarch's children.*

This is the version of history taught to the population — implying a mystical inheritance that is, in truth, artificially manufactured and politically controlled.

---

## Historical Reality (Hidden)

Before the conquest, solar manifestation was not exclusive to nobility. A large portion of the Helian population carried the aptitude. The ruling caste were the strongest, but never the sole wielders.

The exact conditions under which Solarians historically manifested have been lost — even among the modern nobility, who inherited a system they no longer fully understand.

> *Unknown Truth: It is no longer known by the modern nobility when Solarians of the past truly manifested their abilities.*
