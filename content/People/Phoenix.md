## Overview

Phoenix is a Blade of [[The Eclipse Knights]] — the knight to whom [[Marcus]]'s development was assigned, and the closest thing to a parent he remembers from the order. Where [[Lucia Ensis|his mother]] gave Marcus his life, Phoenix gave him the discipline to survive what that life turned out to be.

---

## The Blade

Phoenix completed the Proving long before Marcus was ever smuggled off [[Helios Prime]], and earned the name the order still uses. A **Green Sun** — a high-order manifestation prized for control and perception rather than raw output — Phoenix is a study in precision: a fighter who ends engagements with the smallest possible expenditure, and who regards the volcanic excess of a young [[Solarian]] as a problem to be corrected, not admired.

That made the pairing deliberate. The order does not hand its rarest and most dangerous student to a mentor of the same temperament. It hands him to the one most likely to teach restraint.

---

## Master and Squire

Phoenix took Marcus as an Aspirant and raised him through Squire — years of drills, doctrine, and missions across the outer rim, learning to command the fire he had been born with rather than be commanded by it. Phoenix gave him no special treatment for being a Blue Sun; the order's creed is that potential is not strength, and Phoenix enforced it without exception. Marcus earned every advancement the same way as anyone.

What Phoenix drilled hardest was not how to burn, but how not to. A Blue Sun who loses control does not simply miss — he levels the room, and if he dies in the doing, the blast is measured in geography. So Phoenix taught him to hold the corona at the edge of release and keep it there, for hours if a mission asked it: to be a loaded thing that does not go off until its bearer decides. It is the same discipline that now keeps him still in an Imperial cell — though neither of them could have known, then, what it would one day be for.

Beneath the severity was something the order has no rank for. Phoenix never said it, and Marcus never asked. It did not need to be said.

---

## After the Capture

When Marcus was taken on his way out of Imperial space, the order judged a direct extraction unsurvivable and did not move. Whether Phoenix agreed with that ruling — or simply obeyed it — is not something the order writes down. A Blade does not question the [[Solas|Sovereign]] in the record.

What Phoenix would do, if doctrine and the student ever came into open conflict, remains an unanswered question.
