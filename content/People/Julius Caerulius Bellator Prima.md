## Overview

Julius Caerulius Bellator Prima was the last Hegemon of the [[Helios Hegemony]] before the [[Imperial Conquest]] — the ruler who held the throne through the final wars, refused to capitulate, and was deposed for it.

His cognomen *Prima* marks him as a principal wife's child; his mother's house was [[House Bellator]]. That a Bellator sat the Solar Throne at the moment of collapse is a fact the house has never been allowed to forget.

---

## The Last Loyal Hegemon

By the end of the wars, Julius understood two things his court would not say aloud: that the [[Algalterian Galactic Empire]] could not be stopped by force, and that the betrayal which would end the conflict was already taking shape within the [[Patrilium]] itself.

He did not surrender. When the collaborating faction of the nobility opened its secret negotiations with the Empire, Julius was the Hegemon they had to remove — and they did. He was deposed, his loyal generals isolated and destroyed, and the resistance he represented dismantled from the inside.

---

## The Exile of the Knights

Julius's most consequential act came in the collapse's final hours. Aware of what was coming and unable to prevent it, he arranged for a small number of his most capable [[Solarian]] warriors to be sent beyond Imperial reach before the end. They were not told they were being saved. They were given a mission: survive, preserve what you carry, and do not let the Empire be the last word on what a Solarian is.

Those exiles became the founders of [[The Eclipse Knights]]. Whatever else history records of Julius, that single decision is the reason true Solarian ability survived the purge at all — and, generations later, the reason a child named [[Marcus]] had somewhere to be sent.

---

## Legacy

Within the sanctioned history of the Hegemony, Julius is remembered chiefly as the Hegemon who presided over the fall — and the scorn of that failure attached itself to [[House Bellator]] for centuries. The fuller truth, that he was the last ruler who refused the Empire and the man who preserved the order that still carries the old flame, is known only where it is safest not to say so.
