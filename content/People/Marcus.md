**Sept** is the cognomen given to the child of the sixth concubine — a rank that exists in every house's cycle. Marcus is the seventh child of the seventh family: the last concubine's child in the last house of the cycle, making him among the least statistically likely of all 49 children ever to rule.

**Corona** carries a double meaning: the outermost layer of the sun visible during an eclipse, and the word for *crown*. It was promised to Marcus by [[The Eclipse Knights]] upon his 16th birthday — a name the order considered more honest than the one he was born with.

By completing the Proving he has earned the right to use it.

## Naming Convention

All children produced through the [[Imperial Offering]] share the monarch's family name **Caerulius** alongside their mother's house name. The cognomen reflects birth order by concubine rank:

- **Prima** — main wife's child (first)
- **Sept** — sixth concubine's child (seventh)

Marcus's full formal name: **Marcus Caerulius Bellator Sept**

## Solar Color & Ability

Marcus is a **Blue Sun** — the rarest and most extreme category of [[Solarian]] manifestation, theorized in forgotten ancient texts but never confirmed in the modern era until him.

Where Yellow Sun Solarians must awaken their abilities through a ceremonial process at some point in their development, Blue Suns do not. They are born already with the potential of manifesting naturally. The [[Helion Nodes]] in a Blue Sun operate at an efficiency so far beyond the normal range that the biological boundary between the Helian body and Solarian power effectively does not exist.

## Abilities

Marcus's abilities operate through the standard [[Solarian]] framework of Photon and Graviton attunement — but the baseline from which he operates is anomalous. Where other Solarians must train to unlock their latent abilities, Marcus has them come naturally. His Helion Nodes have been running at Solarian-level output since 12. The Eclipse Knights trained him to control what was already there; they did not build it.

**Photon Attunement** — In Photon mode, Marcus emits. His solar weapon burns with visible heat, his strikes carry concentrated stellar radiation, and at full attunement his corona becomes blinding at close range. His Photon output scales with ambient energy — in direct proximity to a star, his Photon abilities are significantly amplified.

**Graviton Attunement** — In Graviton mode, Marcus pulls. He can distort local gravity, redirect force, and operate as a center of mass that enemies find difficult to move around or away from. His Graviton attunement is less developed than his Photon — the order trained him primarily as an offensive combatant — but it is genuine and growing.

**Solar Weapon** — Marcus manifests a weapon of condensed stellar energy. The form shifts with attunement. In Photon it burns aggressively; in Graviton it becomes dense and heavy, with different mechanical properties. No technological system can replicate the behavior of a genuine solar weapon under stress.

**Radiant Corona** — The phenomenon the order named him for. At significant output levels Marcus produces a visible corona wreath of blue-white solar light. It cannot be concealed at high output. It is both a display of what he is and a practical hazard — the corona is not decorative. 

**Death** — If Marcus dies, the stellar energy accumulated in his Helion Nodes releases in an uncontrolled supernova-scale explosion. The yield scales with his current stellar stage. He cannot prevent this. The Eclipse Knights are aware of it. It is one of the reasons his imprisonment, rather than a quiet execution, is the Empire's chosen approach — the political cost of the explosion in a populated facility would be significant.

The ceiling of his abilities remains unestablished.

## Character

Marcus is direct in the way that people who were raised entirely outside political society tend to be — not rude, but constitutionally uninterested in the kind of indirect communication that the Hegemony's court culture treats as basic courtesy. The Eclipse Knights valued honesty about capability above almost everything else, and that value is visible in how he carries himself.

He is not defined by his origin as a prince. He spent so little of his life inside that identity that it functions more as a second face than a true one. What formed him was the order — its discipline, its philosophy, its demand that worth be demonstrated rather than claimed.

He is patient in the way that people trained to wait for the right moment are patient, and direct in the way that people trained to end engagements quickly are direct. These are not in tension. They are the same quality expressed in different contexts.

What moves him, underneath the discipline, is something the order gave him no framework for: a loyalty to a people he barely remembers, and a quiet, persistent conviction that what was done to them was wrong in a way that demands answering.

## Origins

Marcus was born on [[Helios Prime]] as the seventh child of [[House Bellator]] in the current succession cycle. His birth was unremarkable by noble standards, another child produced through the [[Imperial Offering]]. He was born with silver hair and violet eyes, a rare pairing among the great houses, though neither trait alone was unheard of.

Like all children of the succession cycle, Marcus was raised alongside dozens of noble siblings and rivals. Between the Bellator bloodline, collateral branches, and the children of allied houses, he grew up among nearly fifty brothers and sisters ranging from openly hostile to uncomfortably affectionate. In the Hegemony, noble children were never allowed solitude for long.

At the age of three they entered the academy system together, receiving the finest private instruction the empire could provide. Strategy, history, mathematics, diplomacy, combat, etiquette, and military doctrine were drilled into them relentlessly. Competition between heirs was expected. Mercy was not.

What no one expected was Marcus.

The seventh child of the cycle was never supposed to stand above the others. Yet year after year his aptitude became impossible to ignore. He learned faster, fought harder, and adapted quicker than children bred for greatness long before his conception.

The resentment came quickly.

Some siblings challenged him openly. Others learned to smile before plotting behind his back. Their families were little different. Noble courts were polished things, but never kind.

Marcus endured much of it through the companionship of several children from [[House Aegis]] and [[House Vox]], though most of their names have long faded from memory. One never did.

Juno Caerulius Aurum Prima.

The two became inseparable during their early years, spending countless hours together in the estate libraries, combat simulations, and formal banquets. Affection bloomed quietly between them, in defiance of every layer of noble expectation and political scrutiny around them. At the Succession Cycle's Tenth Year Ball, held to celebrate the coming maturity of the candidate children, they shared their first kiss beneath the stained solar glass of the Bellator ballroom.

In their eleventh year the children underwent their family gene treatments. Some adapted poorly. Others flourished.

Marcus’ results appeared average.

The treatments enhanced his physique, sharpened his reflexes, and accelerated his cognition, but nothing about the results suggested greatness. If anything, it briefly eased the concerns surrounding him.

That illusion shattered a year later.

At the conclusion of their twelfth year the candidates entered the Proving Tournament, a brutal combat trial designed to assess physical capability and tactical instinct. Marcus emerged victorious, though not unscarred. Several relatives left permanent marks across his body before the tournament’s end.

Still, first place was first place.

By the end of the celebration many within [[House Bellator]] had quietly begun accepting what once seemed impossible.

Marcus Bellator was becoming the likely heir.

That same night, something changed.

After the feasting ended and the estate fell silent, Marcus felt a strange pressure building beneath his skin. Heat pooled within his chest. His pulse became erratic. Every instinct told him something inside his body was awakening, something ancient and violent that had waited years for the right spark to emerge.

The sensation only grew stronger.

Near midnight the western wing of the Bellator Estate erupted in blue fire.

The explosion killed dozens of servants instantly and wounded several noble children. At the center of the devastation lay Marcus alone, surrounded by warped metal, molten stone, and burning glass.

Moments later his mother found him.

A faint blue aura radiated from his body. His skin burned hot enough to blister flesh at a touch.

She understood immediately what had happened.

Her son was a true [[Solarian]].

Not merely one of the diluted noble bloodlines that claimed descent from ancient solar sovereigns, but something far older and far more dangerous. A mythical variant spoken of only within the private circles of the high nobility. The sort of being erased from public record. The sort that threatened the balance of the [[Helios Hegemony]] itself.

The sort executed before they could mature.

Knowing the fate awaiting him, Marcus’ mother hid him in secret beneath the estate while the aftermath of the explosion consumed the household. [[House Bellator]] had long maintained covert contact with [[The Eclipse Knights]], quietly feeding them intelligence on the empire and hegemony for generations.

There was only one way to save her son.

She staged his death, allowing the house to believe he had been disintegrated in the center of the blast alongside the servants and attendants killed in the fire. Before dawn, Marcus was smuggled away from [[Helios Prime]] and delivered into the custody of [[The Eclipse Knights|Eclipse Knights]], the only faction capable of concealing and training someone like him.

> She was executed shortly afterward for failing to protect the Bellator heir apparent. In the eyes of the Hegemony, that failure stripped away her final usefulness.

Marcus never learned the full truth of his mother’s death.

Only that she sacrificed herself so he could live.

That has always been enough for him.

And never enough.

## Exile & Training

Marcus came to [[The Eclipse Knights]] as a young child — old enough to remember the nobility and status of Helios Prime, but young enough to be shaped entirely by the order. He trained as an Aspirant, then a Squire, developing under the mentorship of a Blade named Phoenix assigned to his development.

He received no special treatment despite being a Blue Sun. The order acknowledged that he held the potential to change worlds — but potential is not the thing they measure. He lacked what matters most: strength. Should he one day earn it, the order would welcome him as a leader and an ally. Until then, he would earn everything else the same way as anyone.

As a Squire he went on countless missions with Phoenix, training and learning his own abilities while helping maintain a rough peace in the outer rim. The order did this under no pretext of altruism — it is simply easier to justify testing your strength against those who have bad public relations.

He was eventually dispatched on his Proving — a solo trial into Imperial space to locate, engage, and defeat a designated target. He succeeded. On his way out of Imperial territory, he was identified and apprehended by Imperial forces. 

By completing his trial he earned the name Corona within the order, reflecting their own history and his future potential.

---

## Imprisonment

The Empire chose not to execute him. The political reasoning is documented in [[The Eclipse Treaty]] — executing a known Eclipse Knight member, particularly one with genuine Solarian ability, would constitute a provocation the Empire currently considers unnecessary.

He is held as **Inmate A-7527**. His abilities are not publicly acknowledged by Imperial administration. He is officially categorized as a dangerous individual of interest, with his Solarian status classified.

The Eclipse Knights are aware he was captured. They have not moved to retrieve him — not because they have abandoned him, but because the order thinks in terms of what is survivable, and a direct extraction is not.

## What He Knows & Doesn't Know

Marcus knows:
- That he was born a prince and smuggled out as a child
- That genuine Solarian powers make him a target of the Hegemony
- That the Eclipse Knights were founded by exiles from the wars of conquest
- That his homeland is occupied and its people deceived
- That the modern nobility's solar powers are technologically imitated
- That Solarian manifestation was once widespread among ordinary Helians
- How true Solarians historically manifested — the knowledge was destroyed but retained by the Eclipse Knights.

Marcus does not know:
- The details of his mother's execution
- The full terms of [[The Eclipse Treaty]]
- The current affairs of the Hegemony
