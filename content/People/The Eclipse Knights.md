## Overview

**Current Sovereign**: [[Solas]]

The Eclipse Knights are a hyper-militaristic order of [[Solarian]] warriors operating beyond the borders of the Algalterian Galactic Empire in the outer fringes of known space. Founded by survivors of the [[Imperial Conquest]], the order exists for a single purpose: to preserve true Solarian ability, refine it to its highest possible expression, and ensure it is never again extinguished by those who fear it.

The order is obsessed with strength — not as an end in itself, but as the only honest measure of worth in a universe that respects nothing else. Weakness is not condemned. It is simply not accommodated.

Over the centuries their society has grown into [[Penumbra]] — a sprawling space habitat orbiting a blue sun, where Helians are still born, and still learn to manifest Solarian power, though on a minuscule scale compared to the height of the Helios Hegemony.

---

## Origins

When the last loyal Hegemon [[Julius Caerulius Bellator Prima]] recognized that the wars were lost and betrayal was coming from within, he arranged for a small number of his most capable Solarian warriors to be sent beyond Imperial reach before the final collapse. These soldiers were not told they were being saved. They were given a mission: survive, preserve what you carry, and do not let the Empire be the last word on what a Solarian is.

They never returned home. Over generations, in the outer fringes beyond Imperial territory, they built a culture around the only things they had left — their abilities, their discipline, and their absolute refusal to diminish.

The order takes its name from the eclipse: the moment when the sun is obscured but not extinguished, when something powerful is hidden behind something larger — and when the corona becomes visible precisely because the blinding center is covered. It is a name chosen with full awareness of what they lost, and full confidence that what was lost can be reclaimed.

## Philosophy

The Eclipse Knights believe that Solarian power is not a gift. It is not divine favor or noble birthright. It is the result of what a person is willing to become — and it demands everything in exchange for what it offers.

Strength, in the order's doctrine, is the only truth that cannot be falsified. Politics can lie. History can be rewritten. Technology can imitate. But genuine power — the kind that burns from inside a person and reshapes the world around them — cannot be manufactured. This is why the order despises the Hegemony's nobility above all other targets: not for their collaboration, but for their imitation.

The order holds no formal religious structure, but the blue sun of [[Helios Prime]] occupies a near-sacred position in their culture. It is what they came from. It is what they are trying to return to.

## Structure & Ranks

The order is organized around a strict hierarchy of demonstrated ability. Title is earned, never inherited. Upon joining the order aspirants lose the right to a name, only gaining one once they have completed The Proving. 

**Sovereign** — The order's supreme commander. Holds authority over all doctrine, strategy, and major decisions. A Sovereign serves until death or until formally challenged and defeated by a member of Blade rank or above.

**Blade** — Full knights of the order. Have completed the Proving and demonstrated both Solarian ability and sufficient mastery to be trusted with the order's operations. Blades may be assigned missions, lead squire units, or operate independently.

**Squire** — Trainees who have shown enough promise to be accepted into formal training but have not yet completed the Proving. Squires are assigned to Blades for mentorship and are not considered full members of the order.

**Aspirant** — Individuals who have come to the order seeking membership and are being evaluated. An Aspirant who shows no Solarian aptitude within a defined period is respectfully turned away. The order cannot train what isn't there.

---

## The Proving

Every squire who seeks full membership must complete [[The Proving]] — a solo trial dispatched by the order. The nature of the target varies; what does not vary is that the squire must locate, engage, and defeat a target judged by the order to be a meaningful test of their current ability. There is no time limit. There is no assistance.

Squires who return having succeeded are elevated to Blade. Squires who do not return are mourned and remembered. Squires who return having failed to complete the trial may attempt again after a period of further training — but only once.

[[Marcus]] completed his Proving, defeating his target in Imperial space, before his capture by Imperial forces on the way out.

## Political Standing & The Eclipse Treaty

The order operates in permanent cold tension with the Algalterian Empire. They are not powerful enough to threaten the Empire directly, and they know it. Open confrontation would end the order and accomplish nothing for the people they ultimately exist to serve.

Under the [[The Eclipse Treaty]], the Empire agreed not to treat Eclipse Knight activity as grounds for punitive action against the Hegemony, provided certain conditions are met. The practical effect is a kind of mutual non-escalation: the order does not strike Imperial targets at a scale that would demand a military response, and the Empire handles its Eclipse Knight problems quietly rather than with overwhelming force.

Marcus's imprisonment rather than execution is a direct product of this arrangement.

---

## Long-Term Mission

The order has never forgotten why it was founded. Their long-term goal is the liberation of [[Helios Prime]] — the exposure of the nobility's manufactured powers, the restoration of true Solarian ability to the Helian people, and the dismantling of the Imperial arrangement that has governed their homeland for centuries.

They are not close to achieving this. They know that. The order thinks in generations.
