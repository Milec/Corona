## Overview

Juno Caerulius Aurum Prima is a daughter of the current succession cycle — the principal wife's child of [[House Aurum]], and by every measure a leading candidate for the Solar Throne. To the court, she is poise and brilliance in equal measure: the golden daughter of the Hegemony's wealthiest house. To [[Marcus]], for the few short years their lives overlapped, she was something the court had no word for.

---

## The Golden Candidate

As a *Prima*, Juno holds the highest birth rank her house can offer — the child of [[House Aurum]]'s Principal Wife, Lyris Aurum, who sits in the [[Matrilium]]. She was born to the expectation of the throne and raised to carry it.

House Aurum cultivates economic intuition, persuasion, and long-horizon strategy in its bloodline, and Juno inherited all three in abundance. She reads a room the way her house reads a market, and she has never yet met a rival she could not either charm or outlast. Among the forty-nine, she is consistently counted near the front of the field.

---

## Marcus

They found each other early — two children among nearly fifty, thrown together in the estate libraries, the combat simulations, and the endless formal banquets of the [[Heir Candidacy System]]. Where the court taught every candidate to smile before striking, Marcus simply said what he meant, and Juno, who had been raised to distrust exactly that, found she could not look away from it.

Affection grew quietly between them, against every layer of noble expectation. At the Succession Cycle's Tenth Year Ball, held to mark the coming maturity of the candidates, they shared their first kiss beneath the stained solar glass of the Bellator ballroom.

Two years later, the western wing of the Bellator estate erupted in blue fire, and Marcus was gone — disintegrated in the blast, the court was told, along with the servants who died that night.

Juno has no reason to believe otherwise. She grieved a boy the Hegemony insists never became anything worth remembering.

---

## Now

Years on, Juno remains among the strongest candidates of her cycle — luminous, formidable, and increasingly difficult to read. Whether the ambition that now defines her filled the space Marcus left, or merely covers it, is a question she does not appear to ask aloud.

She does not know that he lives. She does not know what he was, or why he truly vanished, or that the official history she was handed was written to make certain no one like him is ever remembered. What she would do with any of it — were the truth to reach her — is not a thing anyone can yet answer, least of all her.
