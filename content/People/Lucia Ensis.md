## Overview

Lucia Ensis was the sixth concubine of [[House Bellator]] to the current Hegemon, and the mother of [[Marcus]]. She is the reason he is alive. She is also the reason he will never know the whole of what she did.

---

## The Sixth Concubine

Like every noblewoman bound to the throne, Lucia came to the Imperial Court through the [[Imperial Offering]] — one of the seven daughters [[House Bellator]] sends to each new Hegemon. As the sixth concubine, she bore a single child, and that child took the cognomen that marks his rank in the cycle: **Sept**.

She raised her son inside the same machine that raised all the candidates — the tutors, the trials, the quiet cruelty of a court that measures children against one another before they are old enough to understand the stakes. What set Lucia apart from the other mothers of the cycle was what she carried and never spoke of: House Bellator's long, covert relationship with [[The Eclipse Knights]], maintained in secret across generations. She knew there was a world beyond the Hegemony's official history. She simply never expected to need it.

---

## The Night of Blue Fire

When the western wing of the Bellator estate erupted in blue flame, it was Lucia who found her son at the center of it — a faint blue aura radiating from his body, his skin hot enough to blister flesh at a touch.

She understood immediately, and completely, what had happened. Her son was a true [[Solarian]] — not one of the diluted noble bloodlines that only claim descent from the old solar sovereigns, but the real thing: the kind of being the Hegemony erases from record and executes before it can mature.

So she did the only thing that could save him. She hid him beneath the estate while the household reeled from the fire, and she staged his death — letting House Bellator believe he had been disintegrated at the heart of the blast, alongside the servants and attendants who died that night. Before dawn, through the channels her house had kept open for generations, she delivered him into the hands of the Eclipse Knights, the one faction capable of concealing and training what he had become.

---

## The Price

> She was executed shortly afterward, for failing to protect the Bellator heir apparent. In the eyes of the Hegemony, that failure stripped away her final usefulness.

[[Marcus]] never learned the full truth of his mother's death. Whether she acted alone in a single desperate night, or whether the escape had been prepared long in advance with the order she quietly served, is not written anywhere he can reach.

He knows only that she died so that he could live.

That has always been enough for him.

And it has never, for one moment, been enough.
